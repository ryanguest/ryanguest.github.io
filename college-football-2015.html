College Football Playoff: What if Michigan had beat Michigan State?

I just finished reading Joseph Blue's excellent post: College Football Playoff: Did the Committee Get it Right? Joseph applies machine learning to the college football scores for the 2015 season and generates his own rankings.

One of the first things I thought (after seeing Stanford in and Oklahoma out of the four team playoffs) was what would happen if Michigan had beaten Michigan State earlier in the season? College football fans will remember that on October 17th, Michigan State beat Michigan 27-23 on a final play fumble return.

If luck hadn't been on Michigan State's side for that game, would things have turned out differently in the end?

Michigan State returns a fumble on the final play of the game.

Data Analysis

I forked a copy Joseph Blue's repository and started to get my hands dirty. I just started learning Octave while taking Andrew Ng's machine learning class on Coursera, so I was excited to put what I had learned to use. You can see my modifications to the repo here: https://github.com/ryanguest/CollegeFootball2015.

After changing the score of the Michigan vs. Michigan State game to 23-21 and setting Michigan as the winner I re-ran the machine learning algorithm.
